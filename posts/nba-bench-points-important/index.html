How important are bench players in the NBA REALLY?

nba
basketball
stats
Author

Nick Clark

Published

April 10, 2026

Among all the discourse that surrounds the NBA, most people tend to overlook the bench players. But they do matter, right? I mean, why else would the NBA have a Sixth Man of the Year award? A lot of people might say that having a deep bench is important for winning championships, while others will say that an All-Star starting five is what matters. So let’s figure out just how important bench players are in the NBA.

One of the most important things a bench player can do is come into the game and help produce points. To do this, we are going to be looking at the past 20 seasons of NBA basketball and see how many points that bench players contributed. We will be using hoopR to pull the total points scored by bench players since 2005.

The first question that we’ll ask is how bench scoring has changed over the course of 20 years. So here is what has changed.

bench_points <- nba_player_stats |>
  filter(starter == FALSE) |>
  filter(did_not_play == FALSE) |>
  group_by(team_name) |>
  summarise(total_bench_points = n()) |>
  filter(total_bench_points >= 6000) |>
  arrange(desc(total_bench_points))

bench_points_over_time <- nba_player_stats |>
  filter(starter == FALSE) |>
  filter(did_not_play == FALSE) |>
  group_by(season) |>
  summarise(total_bench_points = n()) |>
  filter(total_bench_points >= 6000) |>
  arrange(desc(total_bench_points))

team_wins <- team_stats |>
  filter(team_winner == TRUE) |>
  group_by(team_name) |>
  summarise(total_wins = n()) |>
  filter(total_wins >= 450) |>
  arrange(desc(total_wins))

top_bench <- season_rankings_data |>
  select(team_name, season, season_bench_points) |>
  mutate_at(vars(-team_name), as.numeric) |>
  group_by(team_name) |>
  rename(Team = team_name) |>
  rename(BenchPointsScored = season_bench_points) |>
  rename(Season = season) |>
  select(Team, BenchPointsScored, Season) |>
  arrange(desc(BenchPointsScored)) |>
  ungroup()

top_bench_new <- top_bench |> top_n(wt = BenchPointsScored, n = 10)
